The Japanese Garden of Calgary

"Joy in the Snow" a.k.a. 雪の中の喜び

Executive Summary

The Japanese Garden of Calgary envisions a transformative cultural and environmental landmark, celebrating the city's diversity while enhancing its unique identity. By blending Calgary's urban landscape with traditional Japanese horticultural design and aesthetics, the garden will provide a soothing space for residents and visitors alike.

This brief proposal outlines the project's vision, goals, and benefits, including its role in fostering community engagement, promoting environmental sustainability, and offering a unique cultural attraction. Initially, we will focus on building a demonstration garden to showcase the project's potential and attract in-kind contributions of land, expertise, and materials. Through strategic partnerships, volunteer efforts, and phased development, this garden will stand as a lasting legacy for future generations.

Stakeholders

  • Local Residents and Businesses
  • Community Members and Associations
  • Local and Potential Visitors to the Garden
  • Local Government and Regulators
  • Volunteers, Professionals and Collaborators
  • In-Kind Donors and Funding Sources

Project Vision and Goals

Main Vision

The Japanese Garden of Calgary ("Joy in the Snow" a.k.a. 雪の中の喜び [Yuki no Naka no Yorokobi]) will serve as a cultural and educational space, where visitors can experience the exquisite design of Japanese gardens while learning about the cultural significance behind them. The garden will provide a quiet getaway within the city limits, and become a hub for cross-cultural exchange and education.

Target Goals

  • Enhance Calgary's Cultural Identity: The garden will celebrate Calgary's multicultural makeup and strengthen the bond with Japanese descendants, making it an iconic cultural landmark.
  • Foster Community Involvement and Well-Being: The garden will provide a space for community events, educational programs, and personal reflection, enhancing the social fabric of Calgary. Design will adhere to evidence-based principles promoting stress reduction and tranquility.
  • Attract Tourism and Boost Local Businesses (Long-Term): The garden will draw visitors year-round, contributing to Calgary's tourism sector and benefiting surrounding businesses. This will be assessed and developed over time, focusing initially on visitor engagement with the demonstration garden.
  • Support Environmental Sustainability: Through the use of eco-conscious landscaping practices, the garden will highlight water conservation, biodiversity, and ecological balance.

In every stone, every plant, and every moment of silence, the Japanese Garden of Calgary will be a tribute to our city's cultural richness and its commitment to harmony with nature.

Cultural and Environmental Impact

Cultural Significance

  • Our Japanese Garden will symbolize Calgary's commitment to multiculturalism, drawing inspiration from Japanese culture's beauty and philosophy. The venue will host events such as Japanese festivals, tea ceremonies, art exhibitions, workshops, traditional performances, cultural exchange programs, calligraphy and ikebana classes, cooking sessions, Japanese-English immersion, film screenings, seasonal celebrations like cherry blossom viewing, remote cultural exhibitions, and Zen practices, all fostering cross-cultural understanding. We will collaborate with Japanese cultural organizations, including the Japanese Cultural Association of Calgary, the North American Japanese Garden Association, and the Consulate-General of Japan in Calgary, to ensure continued relevance.
  • The garden will also offer educational programs focused on Japanese horticultural principles, philosophy, and history, with hands-on training in traditional pruning, planting, and maintenance techniques. In addition, it will serve as a gathering space for cultural organizations, fostering stronger community ties and promoting cross-cultural engagement.

Environmental Sustainability

  • The Japanese Garden of Calgary will showcase sustainable landscaping practices, such as drought-resistant plants, rainwater harvesting, and low-water design elements like dry gardens (karesansui). These efforts will align with the City of Calgary's environmental goals and serve as a model for future urban development.
  • The design will create a biodiverse environment that supports native plants and provides habitats for urban wildlife, enhancing Calgary's green spaces and fostering an appreciation for nature.

Community and Economic Benefits

Tourism and Economic Impact (Long-Term)

  • The Japanese Garden of Calgary offers significant long-term potential to boost Calgary's attractions and contribute to the local economy. While a detailed economic impact assessment is premature, we anticipate increased tourism, revenue for local businesses, and enhanced property values over time. Our initial focus will be on creating a high-quality, culturally enriching experience that draws visitors and generates positive word-of-mouth through the demonstration garden.
  • Local tourism will benefit from a diversified offering of cultural experiences, complementing other major attractions like the Calgary Stampede. The garden will also appeal to tourists from Japan, creating international connections and boosting global awareness of Calgary as a culturally rich city.

Community Engagement and Wellness

  • The Japanese Garden of Calgary will be designed to support mental well-being, reduce stress, and encourage relaxation. We will apply evidence-based design principles informed by research on the positive impact Japanese gardens have on mental health. The design will feature ponds and streams, carefully selected plants, and quiet, contemplative spaces. To ensure the garden's design and activities truly support well-being, we'll work with experts in horticultural therapy and mindfulness practices.
  • In addition, the garden will be a place for social connection, bringing together people from all backgrounds to learn, celebrate diversity, and enjoy the beauty of nature. It will also help position Calgary as one of the most livable cities in the world.

Partnership Opportunities

Collaborative Partnerships

  • Local businesses, cultural organizations, city officials, and educational institutions will have the opportunity to collaborate with the Japanese Garden of Calgary. This may include hosting cultural festivals, educational workshops, and art exhibitions that enhance the city's cultural offerings.
  • The garden also opens doors for partnerships with Japanese cultural organizations, bringing in experts from Japan to contribute to the garden's design, programming, and events, ensuring an authentic experience for visitors.

Sponsorship and Volunteer Contributions

  • Businesses can sponsor key features of the garden, such as stone lanterns, bridges, or ponds and streams, allowing them to gain lasting visibility and association with a respected cultural landmark. This partnership will demonstrate corporate commitment to cultural diversity, sustainability, and community engagement.
  • Volunteers and skilled professionals can donate their time, expertise, and materials, making a direct contribution to the success of the garden and ensuring it is a collaborative community effort. These contributions will also be publicly recognized, providing professional visibility.

Infrastructure and Site Selection

Site Selection and Integration

  • Our Japanese Garden will ideally be located within the City of Calgary limits, exploring opportunities near or within existing city parks. The design will ensure seamless integration into Calgary's green spaces, with accessible pathways, well-maintained gardens, and educational features that complement the existing urban landscape. Public transportation and parking will be factored into the planning to ensure the site is easily accessible for both locals and tourists.

Demonstration Garden: A Starting Point

  • To demonstrate the feasibility and community support for the Japanese Garden of Calgary, we seek securing in-kind contributions, including a suitable plot of land and the expertise of volunteer professionals. Instead of a full-scale garden, we propose creating a demonstration garden or concept garden on a smaller scale. This pilot project will showcase key design elements, sustainable landscaping practices, and the overall aesthetic of the proposed garden. It will serve as a tangible example of the project's vision and potential, making it easier to attract further support.

Funding and Financial Considerations

Funding Strategy (Phased Approach)

  • The Japanese Garden of Calgary will rely on the dedication and expertise of volunteer professionals in landscape architecture, landscaping, horticulture, construction, and other relevant fields. Their in-kind contributions will significantly reduce initial costs and demonstrate community support. We will also solicit donations of materials (plants, rocks, boulders, stones, gravel, wood, concrete, bamboo, etc.) from local businesses and nurseries.
  • Initially, we will focus on securing a location and establishing the demonstration garden through in-kind contributions and small grants. As the project progresses and gains momentum, we will develop a more comprehensive fundraising strategy to secure larger donations and corporate sponsorships.
  • To ensure transparency and accountability, a group of professional accounting volunteers will manage the financial aspects of the project. All cash or monetary donations will be securely held in a trust fund with a reputable financial institution. Financial reports will be available for accounting and tax purposes.

Project Timeline

  • The Japanese Garden of Calgary will be completed in stages, with each phase building upon the last. The first stage will focus on establishing a demonstration garden, showcasing key design elements and engaging the community. Subsequent stages will expand the garden with additional features, including water elements, plantings, and cultural spaces, in alignment with Calgary's growing season. The final stages will complete the garden, incorporating mature plants, cultural components, and educational features. This phased approach ensures steady progress, with flexibility to adapt to seasonal changes and community needs.

How You Can Help: A Call To Action

This project aims at bringing Calgarians, businesses, and professionals together to make it a reality. The Japanese Garden of Calgary requires the support of visionaries, innovators, and community champions. We are currently seeking the following in-kind contributions:

  • Local Landowners: A plot of land (temporary or long-term) for the demonstration garden.
  • Landscape Architects/Designers: Pro-bono design services for the demonstration garden.
  • Horticulturists/Landscapers: Expertise in selecting/planting appropriate plant species for the Calgary climate.
  • Material Suppliers: Donations of plants, rocks, soil, and other landscaping materials.
  • Construction Professionals: Assistance with building pathways, water features, and other structures within the demonstration garden.

A Space for Future Generations — Once built, the garden will be managed by a non-profit organization dedicated to maintaining it for years to come. We propose the name: The Japanese Garden Society of Calgary.
